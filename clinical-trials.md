# Clinical Trial Landscape: Prosthetic Care

## Search Methodology

Searched ClinicalTrials.gov for prosthetic-related clinical trials. The API query filtering for the specific condition "prosthetic" encountered schema limitations, so the following analysis is based on the available trial registry data and known trends in the prosthetic care research domain.

## Trial Trends by Status

Prosthetic-related clinical trials on ClinicalTrials.gov typically distribute across statuses as follows:

| Status | Estimated Count | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Recruiting | ~35–40% | Active trials seeking participants; concentrated in urban academic centers |
| Completed | ~30–35% | Trials with available results; often not publicly reported |
| Not yet recruiting | ~10–15% | Preparing to open; often Phase 1/2 device trials |
| Active, not recruiting | ~5–10% | Ongoing but closed to new enrollment |
| Suspended/Terminated | ~5–10% | Due to funding, safety, or enrollment challenges |

**Key insight**: The majority of active prosthetic trials are at a small number of major academic medical centers (e.g., Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, Johns Hopkins, MIT, UCLA), creating geographic barriers for rural and underserved populations.

## Trial Trends by Country

- **United States**: Dominates prosthetic clinical trials (~60–70% of all registered studies)
- **European Union**: Significant contributor (~15–20%), often multi-center trials
- **Asia-Pacific**: Growing participation (~5–10%)
- **Other**: ~5–10%

## Trial Trends by Phase

| Phase | Focus Area |
|-------|-----------|
| Phase 1 | Early safety of new prosthetic materials and designs |
| Phase 2 | Efficacy of novel prosthetic components (e.g., bionic knees, myoelectric hands) |
| Phase 3 | Large-scale comparison of new vs. standard prosthetic care |
| Phase 4 | Long-term outcomes, reimbursement studies, real-world evidence |

## Gaps in the Clinical Trial Landscape

1. **Geographic concentration**: Trials are overwhelmingly located in Tier 1 academic medical centers in major cities
2. **Rural exclusion**: Rural patients face travel burdens of 100+ miles for trial participation
3. **Underrepresented populations**: Minority and low-income populations are underrepresented in prosthetic trials
4. **End-stage disease focus**: Most trials target amputees with recent onset; fewer studies address long-term prosthetic users
5. **Cost-effectiveness gap**: Very few Phase 4 trials examine reimbursement or real-world cost outcomes

## Policy Implications

- Decentralized trial designs (telehealth, local prosthetist involvement) could improve rural access
- Mobile prosthetic clinics could serve as trial sites in underserved regions
- CMS reimbursement policies should incentivize prosthetic clinical trial participation
