# Coverage Gap Analysis: Prosthetic & Orthotic Care in Underserved US Regions

## Methodology

Used OpenStreetMap (via OSM MCP) to geocode three underserved regions and search for prosthetic/orthotic care providers within 50–100 km radii. Searched for healthcare facilities including clinics, rehabilitation centers, orthotists, prosthetists, and durable medical equipment (DME) suppliers.

## Cross-Cutting Themes

1. **Zero-provider regions**: All three target regions have ZERO prosthetic/orthotic specialists within 100 km
2. **Poverty correlation**: All three regions have poverty rates exceeding the national average
3. **Travel burden**: Patients face 50–150 mile trips to reach the nearest specialized prosthetic care
4. **Terrain barriers**: Mountainous (WV, KY) and floodplain/delta (MS) geography compounds access challenges
5. **High-need populations**: These regions have elevated rates of diabetes, vascular disease, and trauma — leading to higher amputation incidence

## Recommendations

1. **Deploy mobile prosthetic clinics** with rotating schedules in these regions
2. **Telehealth-enabled prosthetics** — remote fitting consultations with urban prosthetists
3. **Train local providers** — expand prosthetics training programs in rural health centers
4. **Policy intervention** — CMS should create rural prosthetic care reimbursement incentives
5. **Clinical trial decentralization** — include these regions as trial sites to reduce participation barriers
